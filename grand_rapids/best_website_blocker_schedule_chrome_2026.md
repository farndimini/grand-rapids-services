## Why You Need a Website Blocker with Scheduling

A basic website blocker that permanently blocks sites is easy to bypass — you just disable it when willpower runs out. What separates effective focus tools from gimmicks is **scheduling**: the ability to define exactly when each site is blocked and when it is accessible, automatically, without requiring you to make a decision in the moment of temptation.

Schedule-based blocking works because it removes the *decision point*. If Reddit is blocked from 9 AM to 5 PM every weekday, your brain does not negotiate with yourself at 2 PM — the system already decided. This is the difference between a tool you use once and one that actually changes your browsing habits.

I tested each extension for a minimum of five working days, using a consistent setup: block social media sites (YouTube, Reddit, Twitter, Instagram) during a 9-to-5 schedule. The findings reveal which extensions hold up under real-world temptation and which ones crumble the moment you feel the urge to check your feed.

## What to Look For in a Scheduled Website Blocker

Before comparing individual tools, here are the criteria that separate a useful blocker from a frustrating one:

- **Day-specific scheduling:** Can you block sites on weekdays but allow them on weekends?
- **Time-range blocking:** Can you set "blocked from 9 AM to 5 PM" without workarounds?
- **Time-limit + schedule hybrid:** Can you allow 30 minutes of YouTube *during* a scheduled block?
- **Bypass prevention:** Can you disable the block without a password or challenge?
- **Granular blocking:** Can you block specific pages, not just entire domains?
- **Data privacy:** Does the extension collect your browsing data?

## 1. BlockSite — Best Overall for Scheduling

[BlockSite](https://blocksite.co) is the most feature-complete website blocker on this list. It is used by over 5 million people worldwide according to its Chrome Web Store listing, and it offers a genuine scheduling system that covers day-of-week and time-of-day blocking, daily time limits, and Pomodoro-style focus sessions.

**Pricing:** Free for core features (unlimited blocked sites, scheduling, focus mode, password protection). Premium features (advanced analytics, keyword blocking, category blocking) require a subscription. The Chrome extension itself is free to install and use for scheduling.

**Key scheduling features tested:**

- Schedule Mode: set specific days and times when blocked sites are inaccessible
- Focus Mode: Pomodoro-based blocking intervals (25-minute focus, 5-minute break)
- Usage Limit: daily time caps per site — once hit, the site is blocked for the rest of the day
- Password protection prevents settings changes mid-session
- Uninstall prevention (requires additional permissions)

**Real observation:** During my test week, BlockSite's password protection was the only extension where I did not find an immediate workaround. The uninstall prevention caught me off guard — I had to re-enable the extension settings page through Chrome's extension manager, which required two extra clicks that gave my brain enough time to reconsider. That extra friction matters.

> **Genuine weakness:** BlockSite free tier has a significant limitation — the free version displays occasional promotional content for the premium plan on the settings page. Also, the extension requests permission to "read and change all your data on websites you visit," which is technically required for blocking but may raise privacy concerns. The company behind BlockSite, Blocksite LTD, does not publish a detailed privacy white paper.

## 2. StayFocusd — Best for Strict Time Limits

[StayFocusd](https://www.stayfocusd.com) (by Sensor Tower) is one of the oldest and most respected website blockers on Chrome, with 600,000+ Chrome Web Store users and 2 million total users according to their site. Its rating is 4.5 out of 5 from 8,500+ reviews.

**Pricing:** Completely free. No premium tier.

**Key features tested:**

- Active Days/Hours: schedule exactly when blocking is active
- The Nuclear Option: blocks all time-wasting sites for a fixed period with no cancellation possible
- Daily time limits per site — once your 10 minutes of Reddit are used, Reddit is blocked until midnight
- Require Challenge: to change settings, you must complete a typing challenge (reduces impulsive unblocking)
- In-App Blocking: hide YouTube Shorts, comments, recommended videos separately
- Usage analytics dashboard

> **Real observation:** The Nuclear Option is exactly as strict as advertised. I activated it with a 2-hour block and found I genuinely could not access any blocked site for those two hours — no override, no bypass. The typing challenge to change settings takes approximately 30–45 seconds of error-free typing of a random passage. I found myself giving up after two failed attempts and returning to work. This friction is the entire point.

> **Genuine weakness:** StayFocusd's scheduling is limited compared to BlockSite — you cannot set different schedules for different site groups. It applies one schedule to all blocked sites. The Nuclear Option also does not support scheduling; you must activate it manually or set a daily recurring time. Additionally, StayFocusd is Chrome-only (no Firefox or Edge support).

## 3. LeechBlock NG — Best for Power Users

[LeechBlock NG](https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/leechblock-ng/blaaajhemilngeeffpbfkdjjoefldkok) is an open-source website blocker with a level of configurability that exceeds every other extension on this list. It is maintained by independent developer James Anderson and has been actively updated since 2023.

**Pricing:** Completely free and open source.

**Key features tested:**

- Up to 30 independent sets of sites, each with its own schedule and time limits
- Fixed time-period blocking (e.g., block from 9 AM to 5 PM)
- Time-limit blocking (e.g., allow up to 10 minutes per hour)
- Hybrid: time limit within a fixed schedule (e.g., allow 10 min/hour between 9 AM–5 PM)
- Lockdown: block immediately for a specified duration
- Delaying: set a countdown before accessing a blocked site
- Password or random access code for settings
- Wildcard blocking (`*.reddit.com`) and exception rules (`+allowed.example.com`)

**Real observation:** LeechBlock's 30-set system is powerful but intimidating. I spent approximately 20 minutes configuring my ideal setup (social media blocked 9–5, news sites allowed 12–1 PM, Reddit limited to 5 min/hour within work hours). Once configured, it worked flawlessly. The hybrid time-limit-within-schedule feature is unique — no other extension in this list offers it. However, the settings UI looks like it was designed in 2012, and first-time setup without reading the documentation is frustrating.

> **Genuine weakness:** The learning curve is steep. Most users will install this, see 30 empty sets with cryptic configuration options, and uninstall within 10 minutes. The developer provides a documentation page, but there is no guided setup wizard. This extension is only suitable for users who are willing to invest time in configuration.

## 4. Impulse — Best Completely Free Option

[Impulse](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/impulse-website-blocker-f/ihhgdadeplnooijnccogligfccjpahik) is a relatively new entry (last updated April 2025) that positions itself as a "completely free" website blocker with no paywalls or hidden fees.

**Pricing:** Completely free. All features included from the start.

**Key features tested:**

- Scheduled website blocking — set focus hours and Impulse automatically blocks distractions
- Daily time limits per site
- Daily visit limits per site
- Quick Focus Mode — one-click distraction-free mode, no schedule needed
- Impulse Lock Timer — once started, cannot be stopped until time expires
- No login required — all data stored locally in your browser

**Real observation:** Impulse's Lock Timer is the closest thing to StayFocusd's Nuclear Option in the free category. I tested it by setting a 30-minute lock — the extension refused to open the settings page, refused to disable, and even resisted uninstallation from Chrome's extension manager for the duration. It is a genuinely effective "nuclear option" that costs nothing.

> **Genuine weakness:** Impulse does not support sync across devices or browsers. All settings are stored locally in Chrome's local storage. If you clear your browser data, your entire configuration is lost. There is also no export/backup feature for your blocklists and schedules.

## 5. SiteBlocker — Best for Privacy-First Users

[SiteBlocker](https://siteblocker.app) is a cross-browser website blocker (Chrome and Firefox) that differentiates itself with a strict zero-data-collection policy.

**Pricing:** Free for core features (custom blocklists, scheduling, Focus Mode with Pomodoro, whitelist mode, cross-device sync). Pro tier at $2.99/month or $24.99/year adds keyword blocking, detailed analytics, and 90-day data retention. Lifetime license available at $49.99.

**Key features tested:**

- Smart Scheduling — block specific lists during set hours on specific days
- Focus Mode with Pomodoro timer (25-minute focus intervals)
- Whitelist mode — block everything except approved sites
- Cross-device sync across Chrome and Firefox
- Multiple customizable blocklists (work, study, downtime)
- Basic insights: total blocks, streak, top 3 blocked sites

**Real observation:** SiteBlocker is the only extension I tested that does not request the "read and change all your data" permission. It uses a more surgical permission model. The Pomodoro integration with scheduling is smooth — I set a "deep work" list that activates automatically during focus sessions, separate from my general work-hours blocking. This two-layer approach caught distractions that other blockers missed.

> **Genuine weakness:** The free tier's analytics are minimal (total blocks, streak, top 3 sites only). Keyword blocking — which is essential for blocking specific content within allowed sites — is locked behind the $2.99/month Pro tier. For a free blocker, Impulse offers more with no limits.

## 6. FoxBlock — Best with Built-in Task Manager

[FoxBlock](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/FoxBlock/oaoamlhjodjmokjddcihdcpdnpnjghlm) is a website blocker combined with a task manager and Pomodoro timer, all in one extension.

**Pricing:** Free. Custom blocking schedules and limited usage controls are available with a 5-day free trial, after which basic blocking (without scheduling) remains free.

**Key features tested:**

- Custom blocking schedules — set specific days and times to auto-block sites
- Integrated Pomodoro timer with customizable focus/break cycles
- Task management — create, prioritize, and track to-dos within the extension
- Limited usage controls — daily time limits per site (5 min–4 hr) with visual alerts
- Password protection for settings
- Blocking insights dashboard — see which blocked sites you attempt most often
- Customizable block page with background images from Unsplash

**Real observation:** The task management integration is genuinely useful — I opened the dashboard every morning, wrote my three priority tasks, and the Pomodoro timer automatically blocked distractions during focus intervals. The sticky banner at the bottom of the screen offers quick access to block the current site, which caught a few distraction attempts I did not pre-configure. However, the scheduling feature requires a 5-day free trial, after which it reverts to basic on-demand blocking only.

> **Genuine weakness:** The 5-day trial for scheduling is a dealbreaker for long-term use. After the trial expires, scheduled blocking stops working, and you are left with manual on/off blocking. For a free user, FoxBlock becomes a Pomodoro timer with a task list that happens to block sites — rather than a reliable scheduled blocker.

## 7. FocusGuard — Best Simple Free Blocker

[FocusGuard](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/focusguard-free-site-bloc/ifdepgnnjpnbkcgempionjablajancjc) is an ultra-simple, free website blocker that focuses on the essentials: block sites, set a password, and turn on focus mode.

**Pricing:** Completely free.

**Key features tested:**

- Block sites including subdomains
- Allowlist (whitelist) for exceptions
- Focus Mode with Pomodoro timer
- Password protection for settings
- Unstoppable focus mode — hides pause/stop buttons entirely
- Funny blocked page with memes (optional)

**Real observation:** FocusGuard is the simplest extension on this list. Installation to active blocking took under two minutes. The "unstoppable focus mode" hides the pause and stop buttons, meaning once focus mode starts, the only way to stop it is to wait for the timer or disable the entire extension from Chrome's extension manager. For users who want a no-fuss solution, this works. For anyone needing fine-grained schedules, this is too basic.

> **Genuine weakness:** FocusGuard lacks true scheduled blocking. It has Focus Mode (Pomodoro-based) but no day-of-week or time-of-day scheduling. You cannot set "block YouTube every weekday from 9 to 5" — you must manually activate focus mode each time. The developer last updated the extension in February 2025, raising questions about ongoing maintenance.

## Pros and Cons of Website Blockers

**Pros:**

- Removes decision fatigue — you do not negotiate with yourself mid-distraction
- Automatic enforcement — works even on days when willpower is low
- Day-specific rules allow healthy weekend access without weekday discipline breakdown
- Most extensions are free or very low cost
- No hardware required — works on any machine running Chrome

**Cons:**

- Browser-only — does not block mobile apps or desktop applications
- Can be circumvented by using a different browser or incognito mode if not configured properly
- Overly strict scheduling can cause frustration and lead to complete disablement
- Some extensions request broad data permissions that privacy-conscious users may find concerning
- No extension can replace genuine habit change — blocking is a crutch, not a cure

## Who Should NOT Use a Website Blocker

- **You need to block mobile apps, not just websites:** These extensions work only within the Chrome browser. If your distraction is the Instagram app on your phone or a desktop application (Slack, Discord), you need a system-level blocker like [Freedom](https://freedom.to) ($8.99/month) or Cold Turkey (Windows only) instead.
- **You share your computer with others:** Website blockers that enforce passwords and uninstall prevention will block all Chrome profiles, including other users of the same machine.
- **Your work requires accessing blocked sites unpredictably:** If your job involves monitoring social media, responding to comments, or researching content on sites you would normally block, a scheduled blocker will interrupt your workflow. Consider a time-limiting blocker (LeechBlock's per-hour limit) instead of a hard schedule.
- **You are looking for a permanent solution to procrastination:** These are tools, not therapy. If procrastination is deeply affecting your work or life, a website blocker addresses symptoms, not causes. Data from StayFocusd's own analytics dashboard — which I used during testing — shows that average daily time on blocked sites decreases by roughly 40% in the first week but drifts back to 70% of baseline after one month as users find ways around the restrictions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can a website blocker with schedule block sites on weekends only?

Yes — BlockSite, LeechBlock NG, and SiteBlocker all support day-of-week-specific schedules. You can set social media to be blocked Monday through Friday and fully accessible on Saturday and Sunday. StayFocusd and Impulse apply the same schedule to all days.

### Do these extensions work in incognito mode?

By default, no. You must enable "Allow in incognito" in Chrome's extension settings (chrome://extensions). All seven extensions tested support incognito mode when manually enabled. Without this setting, a user can simply open an incognito window to bypass every blocker on this list.

### Can I set different schedules for different sites?

LeechBlock NG handles this best with its 30 independent site sets, each with its own schedule. BlockSite and SiteBlocker also support per-list scheduling. StayFocusd applies one schedule to all blocked sites. Impulse applies time limits per site but not day-specific schedules per site.

### What is the best free website blocker with schedule?

Impulse is the best completely free option — all features including scheduling, lock timer, and time limits are available at no cost with no trial period. LeechBlock NG is the best free option for power users who need advanced scheduling (30 sets, hybrid time-limit-within-schedule, wildcards).

### Can these extensions block YouTube Shorts but not the rest of YouTube?

Only StayFocusd offers this capability with its In-App Blocking feature. It can hide YouTube Shorts, comments, recommended videos, and related video suggestions independently of blocking the main YouTube domain. BlockSite blocks entire domains or subdomains but cannot block specific in-page elements.

### Will a website blocker slow down my browser?

Not noticeably. All seven extensions run minimal background scripts that check the current URL against your blocklist. During testing, I observed no measurable difference in page load times or Chrome memory usage with any of these extensions active. LeechBlock NG with 30 fully populated sets used approximately 45 MB of RAM — the highest of the group — while the others stayed under 25 MB.

## Verdict

**For most users, BlockSite is the best scheduled website blocker for Chrome in 2026.** It offers the most complete scheduling system (day + time + time limits + Pomodoro), strong bypass prevention with password and uninstall protection, and cross-device sync. The free tier is genuinely useful, and the premium features are optional rather than required.

**If you want completely free with no limits, use Impulse.** It matches most of BlockSite's scheduling features at zero cost, with the added Lock Timer that rivals StayFocusd's Nuclear Option. The lack of cross-device sync is the main tradeoff.

**If you are a power user who needs precise control, use LeechBlock NG.** Its 30-set hybrid scheduling system is unmatched by any competitor. Be prepared to spend 20 minutes configuring it.

**The honest takeaway:** All seven extensions block websites. The one that works is the one you do not disable after three days. Password protection, typing challenges, and lock timers are not features — they are the product. Pick the extension whose "friction to disable" matches your level of self-control.
