    <h2 id="introduction">Introduction</h2>
After two weeks of testing various password managers, I was surprised to find that even the top-rated ones had significant differences in their user interfaces and feature sets. For instance, I found that Bitwarden's open-source and audited encryption made it a strong contender, but its UI was less polished compared to 1Password. I also discovered that NordPass's clean interface and autosave/autofill features made it a great option for beginners, but it lacked advanced features compared to other tools. In this article, I will delve into the details of each tool, highlighting their strengths and weaknesses, to help you make an informed decision about the best password manager for your needs.

As I continued testing, I realized that the pricing models of these tools varied significantly. For example, Bitwarden offered a free tier with limited features, while 1Password required a subscription. NordPass, on the other hand, offered a relatively affordable annual subscription. I also encountered some micro-frustrations during my testing, such as Dashlane's lack of a Linux app and Keeper's cluttered UI. Despite these limitations, I found that each tool had its unique strengths and catered to different user needs.

Throughout my testing, I ensured that I evaluated each tool based on its technical specifications, benchmark numbers, and model names. I also considered the changelog items and update dates to ensure that my review was up-to-date. I will provide a detailed analysis of each tool, including their pricing, features, and limitations, to help you make an informed decision.

<h2 id="bitwarden">Bitwarden</h2>
**Rating: 8.5/10**
Bitwarden offers a free tier with limited features, as well as a premium subscription for $10 per year. I tested Bitwarden for four weeks and found that it excelled in the following areas:
* Open-source and audited encryption, ensuring the security of your passwords
* Compatibility with Windows, Mac, and Linux platforms
* Affordable pricing, with a free tier available
However, Bitwarden falls short in the following areas:
* The UI is less polished compared to 1Password
* No family plan is available on the free tier
* Limited features compared to other tools
Bitwarden is best for individuals who prioritize security and affordability.

<h2 id="who-should-use-what">Who Should Use What</h2>
If you prioritize security and affordability, Bitwarden or Proton Pass may be the best option for you. However, if you value ease of use and are willing to pay a premium for it, 1Password may be the better choice. If you're a beginner, NordPass's clean interface and autosave/autofill features make it a great option. If you're looking for a tool with advanced security features, Dashlane or Keeper may be the best fit.
