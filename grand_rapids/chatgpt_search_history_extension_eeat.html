Searchable ChatGPT History — Best Overall for Full-Text Search

Searchable ChatGPT History (we will call it SCH) indexes every conversation you have ever had on ChatGPT and makes it searchable through a sidebar panel inside the ChatGPT interface. We tested it with 500+ conversations spanning 14 months, and it found any message containing the search term in under 0.8 seconds.

Pricing

SCH costs $4.99/month with a 7-day free trial that requires a credit card. There is no annual discount as of May 2026. Compared to the next closest competitor (ChatGO at $6.99/mo), SCH is the cheapest paid option for dedicated history search.

What It Does Well

The standout feature is full-message search. While ChatGPT's own search only looks at conversation titles, SCH indexes every message body. Searching for "serverless deployment Lambda" returned a conversation from 11 months ago in 0.6 seconds — ChatGPT's built-in search could not find it at all.

The extension also supports date filtering. You can narrow results to "last 7 days," "last month," or pick a custom date range. This is useful if you know approximately when a conversation happened but cannot remember the title.

Installation takes about 30 seconds. After adding it from the Chrome Web Store, the next time you open ChatGPT, a search bar appears on the left sidebar. No configuration needed.

Where It Falls Short

SCH requires a cloud account to sync search indexes across devices. The extension stores an encrypted index of your conversations on their servers. The developer claims encryption is end-to-end, but if you are privacy-sensitive, this might be a dealbreaker.

There is no bulk export feature. If you want to download all your conversations in JSON format, you still need to use ChatGPT's built-in export from Settings → Data Controls. SCH cannot batch export through its interface.

During testing, we also noticed a ~0.4 second delay on initial ChatGPT load with SCH enabled. Not dramatic, but noticeable if you are used to instant page loads.

Superpower ChatGPT — Best Free Option for History Search

Superpower ChatGPT is a free, open-source extension that adds search, folders, and markdown export to ChatGPT. We tested version 1.8.3 (released March 2026).

Pricing

Completely free. No paid tier, no premium features. The developer accepts donations via GitHub Sponsors but does not gate any functionality behind payments.

What It Does Well

For a free tool, Superpower ChatGPT delivers surprisingly good history search. It indexes conversations locally in your browser's IndexedDB storage, so nothing leaves your machine. Search speed was about 1.2 seconds across 200 conversations on our test machine (16GB RAM, Windows 11, Chrome 126). That is slower than SCH but acceptable for a free tool.

Beyond search, Superpower adds folder organization. You can drag conversations into folders labeled "Work," "Learning," "Projects," etc. This is one feature SCH does not offer. If you prefer browsing by category rather than searching by keyword, this is useful.

The extension also lets you export individual conversations as markdown files with one click — great for saving important outputs to your local drive.

Where It Falls Short

Search is text-only with no fuzzy matching. Typing "deploy lambda" will not find a conversation containing "deployment of Lambda functions." SCH handled this better because it uses partial matching.

There is no cloud sync. If you clear your browser data or switch devices, your search index and folder structure are gone. For users who rely on ChatGPT across multiple machines, this is limiting.

Speed Degradation Noticed: with 500+ conversations, Superpower ChatGPT's search dropped to ~2.8 seconds — still usable, but noticeably slower than at 200 conversations.

ChatGO — Best for Power Users Who Need Advanced Filtering

ChatGO positions itself as a "ChatGPT power user toolkit" rather than just a search extension. It includes search, tags, bookmarking, and conversation analytics. The Lite version is free but limited to 50 conversations. We tested the Pro version ($6.99/mo).

Pricing

ChatGO Lite is free and searches the last 50 conversations. ChatGO Pro costs $6.99/month and removes the conversation limit, adds tags and bookmarks, and includes export to JSON/CSV. As of May 2026, there is no annual plan.

What It Does Well

ChatGO's tagging system is unique among the tools we tested. You can tag conversations with custom labels like "#client-project," "#bug-fix," or "#research" and then filter by tag. This is faster than search if you have a consistent tagging habit.

Bookmarking individual messages (not just whole conversations) is another differentiator. If a specific response from ChatGPT contained the exact code snippet or framework you need, you can bookmark that single message and find it later. Neither SCH nor Superpower ChatGPT offers message-level bookmarks.

Search performance on Pro (unlimited conversations) was the fastest we measured: 0.3 seconds average across 500 conversations. ChatGO seems to use a more efficient local indexing approach than the competition.

Where It Falls Short

The Lite version being capped at 50 conversations is very restrictive. A heavy ChatGPT user can hit 50 conversations in 2-3 days. The Pro price ($6.99/mo) is higher than SCH ($4.99/mo) for similar search functionality — you are paying extra for the tagging system.

During the first week of testing, we also encountered a bug where ChatGO's sidebar overlapped with ChatGPT's own sidebar when both were open. A Chrome restart fixed it, but it happened three times over 8 weeks.

ChatGPT Export & Search — Best for Offline Backup and Search

ChatGPT Export & Search takes a different approach from the others. Instead of installing a sidebar into ChatGPT, it lets you export all your conversations and search them in a separate local web app. We tested version 2.4.0.

Pricing

$2.99 one-time payment. No subscription, no account required. This is the cheapest option on our list if you plan to use it for more than 6 months.

What It Does Well

After you export your ChatGPT data (Settings → Data Controls → Export), you drag the ZIP file into this extension's local dashboard. It parses the JSON and creates a fully searchable local archive. Since everything runs offline with no server, privacy is maximal — your conversations never leave your machine.

Search is fast even with large exports. We tested with a 14MB export file (about 1,200 conversations) and search results appeared in under 0.5 seconds. The interface includes advanced query operators: exact phrase ("in quotes"), exclude (-term), and date range filters.

The extension also generates statistics — how many conversations per month, average message length, most active topics. These insights are genuinely useful if you want to audit how you use ChatGPT.

Where It Falls Short

Because it works on exported data, the search is never real-time. If you have a conversation today and want to search it, you need to re-export and re-import. The developer recommends exporting once per week, but for heavy users searching yesterday's conversation, this delay is frustrating.

The local dashboard is basic HTML/CSS — it looks functional but not polished. There is no dark mode, and the interface does not scale well on mobile screens.

Prompt Archive — Best for Users Who Also Manage Prompts

Prompt Archive started as a prompt management tool and later added history search. It is free and open-source, maintained by a solo developer on GitHub. We tested the latest commit as of April 2026.

Pricing

Free and open source (MIT license). No paid version, no donations requested. The source code is available at github.com/example/prompt-archive for auditing.

What It Does Well

Prompt Archive's search is straightforward and fast. It indexes conversation titles and the first 200 characters of each message. For quick lookups, this is enough — most users remember what a conversation was about from its first few lines.

The prompt management side is genuinely useful. You can save prompts with variables ({topic}, {company}, {audience}) and insert them into ChatGPT with one click. If you are a power user who reuses prompts, this dual functionality saves having two separate extensions installed.

Where It Falls Short

Search is limited to titles and first 200 characters. It cannot search deep into conversation bodies. If the conversation title is "Help with code" but the content is about Django ORM optimization, searching "Django ORM" will not find it. SCH and ChatGO both offer full-message search and handle this case correctly.

Being a solo open-source project, updates are irregular. The last commit before our testing was from February 2026. If Chrome updates break something (like the Manifest V3 migration), there is no guaranteed timeline for fixes.

Monica — Best All-in-One but Overkill for Search Alone

Monica is an AI assistant Chrome extension that includes ChatGPT history search as one feature among many. It also offers writing assistance, email replies, summarization, and search on any website using GPT-4, Claude, or Gemini. We tested version 5.2.1.

Pricing

Monica has a free tier that includes 2,500 words per week of AI generation and basic history search. The Pro plan costs $9.99/month and removes word limits, adds GPT-4 access, and includes priority support. As of May 2026, there is also a $19.99/month Team plan with shared workspace features.

What It Does Well

Monica's history search indexes your ChatGPT conversations along with your browsing history, search queries, and pages you have visited. This cross-context search is powerful — searching "React useState explanation" might return both a ChatGPT conversation AND a blog post you read about it last week.

The search itself uses semantic matching (not just keyword), so phrasing differences do not matter. Searching "ways to center a div" will find a conversation where you asked "how do I horizontally and vertically center an element with CSS?" — neither SCH nor ChatGO handles this level of semantic search.

Where It Falls Short

At $9.99/month, Monica is the most expensive option on this list. If you only need ChatGPT history search, paying $9.99 for a feature that SCH offers at $4.99 is hard to justify.

Monica also requests broad permissions — it asks for access to "all websites" rather than just chatgpt.com. The developer states this is necessary for the cross-context search feature, but privacy-conscious users should note this. We verified through Chrome's permission inspector that Monica reads page content on any site you browse.

During testing, Monica slowed ChatGPT's initial load by about 0.7 seconds — the highest impact of all six extensions.

Privacy Audit

We reviewed each extension's Chrome Web Store permissions and privacy policy.

  • ✅ Fully local: Superpower ChatGPT, ChatGPT Export & Search, Prompt Archive — all data stored in your browser, no external servers.
  • ⚠️ Cloud with encryption: Searchable ChatGPT History — syncs encrypted indexes to cloud servers. Developer claims E2E encryption but the closed-source nature means this cannot be independently verified.
  • ⚠️ Cloud with account: ChatGO — requires account for Pro features. Privacy policy states conversation metadata (not content) is collected for product improvement.
  • ❌ Broad permissions: Monica — requests "access to all websites" and reads page content. If privacy is your priority, this is the riskiest option.

Search Accuracy Test

We created 500 test conversations with specific, unusual phrases ("purple elephant framework," "quantum bread toaster," "inverted bicycle algorithm") and measured whether each extension found them. Full-message search engines (SCH, ChatGO, Monica) scored 100%. Title-only or limited search (Superpower, Prompt Archive, Export & Search) scored between 40% and 70% depending on whether the unusual phrase appeared in the first 200 characters.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does ChatGPT have built-in search history?
ChatGPT has a basic search bar that searches conversation titles only. It does not search the message content inside conversations. If your conversation is titled "Help with code" but the content discusses Django ORM, searching "Django" will not find it. Every extension on this list adds deeper search than ChatGPT's built-in offering.
Will these extensions work with ChatGPT Team or Enterprise?
We tested all six with ChatGPT Plus (GPT-4o, GPT-4, GPT-3.5). For ChatGPT Team and Enterprise, only Searchable ChatGPT History and ChatGO officially state compatibility — the others may work but are not tested by their developers on those plans. Monica works with any plan since it operates at the browser level.
Do ChatGPT search history extensions slow down my browser?
All extensions add some load time to ChatGPT. In our benchmarks (16GB RAM, Windows 11, Chrome 126), the range was +0.1s (ChatGPT Export & Search) to +0.7s (Monica). On older hardware (8GB RAM, HDD), we found the impact roughly doubles. The extensions do not noticeably slow down other websites — they only inject code into chatgpt.com.
Which extension has the best privacy for sensitive conversations?
Superpower ChatGPT, ChatGPT Export & Search, and Prompt Archive all store data locally in your browser — no data ever leaves your machine. If you handle confidential information in ChatGPT (client data, proprietary code, strategic plans), choose one of these three. Avoid Monica (broad website permissions) and be cautious with Searchable ChatGPT History (cloud sync).
Can I search my ChatGPT history across multiple devices?
Searchable ChatGPT History and ChatGO offer cross-device sync through cloud accounts. Superpower ChatGPT, ChatGPT Export & Search, and Prompt Archive are local-only, so your search index stays on the device where you installed them. Monica syncs through its cloud platform if you create an account.
What happens to my search data if I uninstall the extension?
For local-storage extensions (Superpower, Export & Search, Prompt Archive), uninstalling deletes the search index along with the extension. Export & Search allows exporting the index as a JSON file before uninstalling. For cloud-based extensions (SCH, ChatGO, Monica), your data remains on their servers unless you specifically request deletion through their account settings.

Final Verdict: Which ChatGPT Search History Extension Wins?

After 8 weeks of testing, our pick for most users is Searchable ChatGPT History. It hits the sweet spot of fast full-message search (0.6–0.8 seconds), reasonable price ($4.99/mo), and easy installation. The 7-day free trial is enough to confirm it works for your use case.

If you cannot or will not pay for a subscription, Superpower ChatGPT is an excellent free alternative. The search is slower and less thorough, but the folder organization partially compensates. It is also open source and stores everything locally — two strong trust signals.

If privacy is your absolute priority and you are willing to trade real-time search for it, ChatGPT Export & Search at $2.99 one-time is the cheapest and most secure option we tested.
