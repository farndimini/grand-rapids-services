## Common Plumbing Emergencies by Season

Grand Rapids' four-season climate creates predictable (and preventable) patterns:

### ❄️ Winter (Dec–Feb)
- **Frozen pipes** — Most common when temps drop below 15°F. Attics, crawlspaces, and exterior walls at highest risk.
- **Ice dam leaks** — Melting snow backs up under shingles, mimics roof leak.
- **Furnace + water heater strain** — Cold inflow water increases sediment buildup.

### 🌸 Spring (Mar–May)
- **Sewer line backups** — Heavy rain + thawing ground = overloaded municipal lines.
- **Sump pump failure** — Spring thaw activates pumps that failed unnoticed during winter.
- **Outdoor spigot leaks** — Frost-damaged hose bibs crack when water is turned on.

### ☀️ Summer (Jun–Aug)
- **Drain clogs** — Increased usage from guests, kids home from school, parties.
- **AC condensate line clogs** — Leads to water backup and ceiling damage.
- **Water heater overwork** — Groundwater enters at warmer temps, harder on aging units.

### 🍂 Fall (Sep–Nov)
- **Water heater sediment** — Seasonal maintenance window before winter demand.
- **Gutter/downspout clogs** — Misdirected water mimics foundation leaks.
- **Gas line checks** — Heating season startup reveals cracks in gas supply lines.

> **Pro tip:** Schedule a [fall plumbing inspection](https://www.epa.gov/watersense/fall-home-maintenance-tips) before Thanksgiving. Most emergency calls during the holidays are preventable.

## What to Do While Waiting for the Plumber

When a plumbing emergency strikes, every minute counts. Here is the **immediate-action checklist** we give every customer:

1. **Shut off the water** — Locate your main shutoff valve (usually near the water meter in the basement or crawlspace). Tag it now so you can find it in the dark.
2. **Turn off the water heater** — If the leak is near the water heater or you are shutting off main water, flip the breaker (electric) or set gas valve to PILOT to prevent burn-out.
3. **Open a faucet** — Open a cold water faucet on the lowest floor to drain remaining line pressure and reduce flooding.
4. **Move valuables** — Clear furniture, electronics, and rugs from the affected area.
5. **Document damage** — Take photos for insurance purposes before we begin work.
6. **Call us** — **(616) 555-0147** — We will talk you through additional steps specific to your situation.

> The [City of Grand Rapids](https://www.grandrapidsmi.gov/living-in-gr/water-and-sewer-services) recommends homeowners locate and tag their shutoff valve during spring and fall maintenance. If you live in a pre-1950 home (common in Heritage Hill, Eastown, and Midtown), your shutoff may need professional servicing.
